Pomeranian Dog

Descended from large sled dog breeds, the now-tiny Pomeranian has a long and interesting history. The foxy-faced dog, nicknamed “the little dog who thinks he can,” is compact, active, and capable of competing in agility and obedience or simply being a family friend.

Rottweiler Dog

The Rottweiler was originally dogs bred to drive cattle to market. Later they were used to pull carts for butchers. .

Shorkie Dog

Shorkies are mixed-breed dogs. They are not purebreds like their Shih Tzu or Yorkshire Terrier parents.Shorkies usually have straight, medium-to-long coats, and they're generally considered to be a good choice for allergy sufferers.

German Shepherd

  • The German Shepherd Dog is one of America’s most popular dog breeds — for good reasons.
  • They’re intelligent and capable working dogs.
  • Their devotion and courage are unmatched. And they’re amazingly versatile.

Valley Bulldog

  1. The Valley Bulldog is a mixed breed dog–a cross between the Boxer and English Bulldog breeds.
  2. Medium in size, active, and loyal, these pups inherited some of the best qualities from both of their parents.
  3. Valley Bulldogs also go by the name Bull Boxer.
